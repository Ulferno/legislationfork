Ranking Structure

Field Cadet | Cap: N/A | Low Command

Field Cadets consist of the newest personnel of the Mobile Task Forces, and have proven themselves worthy of a position through tryouts. Field Cadets main duty is to patrol Epsilon to look for any threats, and to actively assist SD when necessary. Field Cadets can also request to follow a Task Force Specialist+ if theyâ€™d like to assist in their duties.
Requirements:

  • Pass tryouts when hosted.
Task Force Agent | Cap: 14 Per Task Force | Low Command

Task Force Agent is the base rank that all personnel receive once their Field Cadet phase is down, and is at the bottom of the ranking structure. Field Agents may participate in their duties and do not focus on much more other than their duties that are assigned to them.
Requirements:

  • Pass the Field Cadet Phase.
Task Force Specialist | Cap: 6 Per Task Force | Low Command

Task Force Specialist is the 2nd lowest rank within MTFâ€™s hierarchy, and doesn't receive much more from Agents. Specialists are allowed to supervise Field Cadets, and may receive more weapons depending on which task force they are in. Members of this rank are generally trusted and have very fair chances at eventually becoming command members.
Requirements:

  • Has served in their said task force for 2 weeks.
  • Chosen by task force command.
Task Force Sergeant | Cap: 6 Per Task Force | Medium Command

Task Force Sergeants are the first and only Medium Command within MTF. They host trainings for their task force, give out warnings, and work together with their command team to further the progress of their task force. Task Force Sergeants are very well trusted members within the department and get access to more information than other ranks.
Requirements:

  • Has served in their said task force for 4 weeks.
  • Chosen by the task force command.
Task Force Lieutenant | Cap: 2 Per Task Force | High Command

Task Force Lieutenants operate as somewhat of a right hand man to the Task Force Captain, promote and demote members of their task force, as well as monitor their activity. Members of this role have likely been in the Mobile Task Forces for a long time, and have lots of experience.
Requirements:

  • Has served in their said task force for 6 weeks.
  • Chosen by task force command.
Task Force Captain | Cap: 1 Per Task Force | High Command

Task Force Captain is the highest rank one can achieve within a task force. Task Force Captains are in charge of promoting and demoting task force members, as well as managing the general welfare of their task force. Task Force Captains receive Security Class 3, and are extremely trusted personnel within the Mobile Task Forces.
Requirements:

  • Has served in their said task force for 8 weeks.
  • Chosen by the MTF Field Chief & Department Overseer
Field Chief | Cap: 2 | Central Command

Field Chiefs are the department directors of the Mobile Task Forces, managing the welfare of the entirety of the department, opening tryouts, and promoting and demoting task force command members. This rank requires months of hard work to achieve.
Requirements:

  • Has served as a Task Force Captain for 3 weeks.
  • Chosen by the Department Overseer.
